Students for Justice Clothing (2010-2012)

I helped start a non-profit organization that specialized in printing and distribution of sweatshop free clothing that was printed and pressed out of a basement in Mississauga. We had supplied local high schools in Mississauga with gym attire and had an e-commerce website that handled our online sales.

City of Toronto

One of my part-time jobs during high school was working as a ski instructor with the City of Toronto. This job had me working with individuals from a variety of a age groups. This was one of the most pivotal learning experiences that I encountered and has instilled invaluable people skills into my character.

Culinary Involvement

I was in dire need of a part time job in high school and was told that the Kelsey's in my neighbourhood was looking for line cooks. Seriously doubting my culinary skills at the moment, I had started off working my way up the kitchen and eventually got as involved as moving to different restaurants like Montana's and Red Lobster. Eventually working my way to being a part time sous-chef at Earl's.

Engineering Innovations

As an engineering student, I get easily excited when the opportunity to build or design something cool arises.
